Chicken Adobo

Description

Chicken adobo, or adobong manok, is a popular Filipino dish known for its rich, savory, and tangy flavor profile, achieved through braising chicken in a sauce of soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1 head garlic (minced)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 3 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp black peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp brown sugar (optional)
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 1 cup water

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a bowl, combine chicken pieces with soy sauce, vinegar, minced garlic, and peppercorns. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes or overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Heat the oil: In a large pan or pot, heat cooking oil over medium-high heat.
  3. Saut√© aromatics: Add sliced onions and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes.
  4. Brown the chicken: Remove chicken from marinade (reserve the marinade) and brown the pieces on all sides, about 5-7 minutes total.
  5. Add marinade: Pour the reserved marinade over the chicken and add bay leaves.
  6. Add water: Pour in water and bring the mixture to a boil.
  7. Simmer: Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes until chicken is tender and cooked through.
  8. Adjust seasoning: Add brown sugar if desired for a slightly sweet flavor. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.
  9. Reduce sauce: If you prefer a thicker sauce, remove the lid and simmer for an additional 10-15 minutes to reduce the liquid.
  10. Serve: Remove bay leaves and serve hot over steamed rice. Garnish with chopped green onions if desired.
